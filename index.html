Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a lawyer to file the I-129F?

Most couples with straightforward cases (first marriage, no criminal history, clear eligibility) can successfully file the I-129F themselves. This guide is designed for those situations. However, if you have prior denials, criminal history, complex immigration history, or concerns about eligibility, consulting a licensed immigration attorney is strongly recommended.

What if USCIS updates the I-129F form?

You'll receive free updates to the guide whenever USCIS releases a new version of the form. The guide is maintained to reflect current USCIS requirements, and you'll be notified via email if significant changes are made.

What if I'm not tech-savvy?

No technical skills required. The kit includes both web-based templates and downloadable PDF files. You can print everything and work on paper if you prefer. The instructions are written in plain English with no jargon.

Can I print everything?

Yes. All materials are available as print-ready PDFs. You can print the entire guide, all templates, and checklists. Many users prefer working with printed copies, and that's fully supported.

What if I make a mistake?

The guide is specifically designed to help you catch common mistakes before filing. It includes section-by-section checklists, error-prevention tips, and explanations of what USCIS is actually looking for in each field. Following the guide systematically reduces the likelihood of errors significantly.

Will this guarantee my petition is approved?

No one can guarantee approval—not attorneys, not filing services, and not this guide. USCIS makes all final decisions based on your individual circumstances. What this guide does is help you file a complete, well-organized petition that avoids common mistakes and presents your case clearly.

Is this guide up to date?

Yes. The guide is based on the current I-129F form and USCIS requirements as of January 2026. The materials are actively maintained, and you'll receive free updates if USCIS changes the form or requirements.
